ABOUT OUR JOURNY

Nallepilly was agricultural land under old Cochine. Two schools (L.P & U.P) were established for the progress in education before independence people depended the schools in Chittur for high school education. Most of the people of Eastern province stopped their studies due to lack of facilities. A kind hearted person Sri N.S Krishnaayyar realising pathetic situation of the people started the eight standard in the agraharam in 1941 Sri. Krishnanunni Valiya Mannadiar, a member of Chondath family realised the urgent need of a high school in Nallpelly gave free land and in 1942 foundation stone laid for the present Sree Krishana High School. The inauguration of the school building was done by the director of education I.N Menon ( M.A B Lit ) (OX) in 1943. The frame of the school was enriched by Sri. Raveendranath ( I.P.S ), Sri. N.V Krishnan ( I.A.S ) etc. In 1972 Tamil medium was introduced and hostel services were provided. The school started its victorious journey with 23 divisions and more than 1000 students.
